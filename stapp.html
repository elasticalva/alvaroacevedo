STAPP UX - UI Case - The productivity tool

  • by:Álvaro Acevedo
  • Research, Design
This project took place during the UX-UI Bootcamp at IronHack Barcelona.

My role

UX/UI DESIGNER / PLATFORM: IOS (iPhone 8 Plus)
I conducted user research (both quantitative surveys and qualitative interviews), wireframed user flows, designed the UI, and prototyped the flow and key interactions.

Tools: Sketch, some InVision, and lots of Flinto.


The Problem

Have you ever been studying or working and realized that your productivity decreases with your phone at your side? Have you ever been annoyed during a conversation with someone that repeatedly gazes on his phone?
STAPP is a productivity tool that helps to prevent this happen with an easy interface and simple tweaking configuration.

I was inspired to design it after watching the Google Keynote I/O 2018 when they presented "Digital Wellbeing".


The goal

Reduce smartphone usage by implementing a "habit-forming" in the home screen using the minimum steps possible. And this goes through visualizing how much and when they use those apps plus being able to switch them off.


TRANSLATING THIS TO APP DESIGN

Phase 1: UX

To understand how the users will identify this problem and how they will solve it, I surveyed 30+ participants, ages ranging from 16-70, distributed across Spain.

The survey results helped me quantify and observe trends that informed how I thought about the UX for STAPP.


To help me empathize I realized some interviews with the potential users of the application and they revealed key problems that I disregard at first, like setting a timer for determined applications instead of just shutting them off. This is one of the main features now!

All this drives me to Gloria, my user persona.

I feel not being as productive as I can be

Following Gloria through all day you can discover when she’s using her smartphone the most and why.


The User Journey depicts that during afternoons it's hard for her to be productive, she's tired and any little distraction from her phone doesn't help.


The Site Map helped me a lot in solving this process of forming a 'habit-forming' in the home screen (that was the last iteration) As you can see, the home screen composes only of the daily and weekly usage of apps and one CTA button.



This low-fi prototype was what I presented during the first user tests. It allowed me to polish the application with all the feedback received.

One of the best insights I've obtained to make STAPP more straightforward was to skip the log in process in favor of just data exporting/backup.

Phase 2: UI

With this mood board, I wanted to connect the urban feel of clean and solid concrete with two accent colors, to make it easy for the user to read the main info on the home screen in one glimpse.

Final Thoughts

I got feedback thanks to the usability testing and I found many improvements to make to my initial designs that could be developed in future iterations. In this project I enjoyed the phase of UX a lot, I’ve discovered a lot of things related to mobile addiction and how the user feels about this problem that concerns us all.


Conclusion

Iteration is key when you want to improve the usability and efficiency of the application. With extended user research, the user experience while interacting with the product can be a lot more satisfactory and simple.

This is just an MVP, there is a lot more to discover!
